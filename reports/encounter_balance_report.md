# Encounter Balance Report

PF2e XP budget (4 players): **Trivial 40 · Low 60 · Moderate 80 · Severe 120 · Extreme 160**.
Creature XP by level vs party level (PL): PL−4=10, PL−3=15, PL−2=20, PL−1=30, **PL=40**, PL+1=60, PL+2=80, PL+3=120, PL+4=160.
Party-size adjustment: ±~10 XP of budget and ±1 creature step per PC above/below 4.

> Scope: **Chapter 1 — the Old Fishery (Phase-2 pilot)**. Later chapters append below.

### E8 — Boss fight detail (the "Lamm is a Lamb" fix)

**Problem inherited from PF1e:** Gaedren is a deliberately frail old man; played straight he dies in a round and the campaign's emotional climax fizzles (strong community consensus). PF2e's action economy makes a lone frail boss even flatter.

**PF2e design:** make the fight a **Severe** by pairing two PL+1 creatures and making the *crocodile* the real threat, with Gaedren as a slippery catalyst:
- **Gaedren (level 2, PL+1, 60 XP):** frail (HP 30, AC 17), Speed 15 (Limping Gait), sniper from across the pool. **Spur the Beast** (shoot Gobblegut to enrage it) is his opening; **Nimble Dodge** keeps him alive a round or two; flees at ≤8 HP toward the A12 skiffs.
- **Gobblegut = Crocodile (level 2, PL+1, 60 XP):** the muscle. Grab + Aquatic Ambush; drags PCs into the gator pool. Enraged by Spur the Beast (+2 status to attack/damage, must attack nearest non-Gaedren creature).
- **Terrain is load-bearing:** two 5-ft walkways cross the gator pool; reaching Gaedren means crossing the water Gobblegut controls. This is what makes 120 XP *play* like a climax instead of a turkey-shoot.

| Threat assessment | |
|---|---|
| Action economy | ✅ 2 enemies vs 4 PCs (not a lone solo). |
| Swing risk | Moderate. Crocodile Grab → drag into water can spike on a crit; mitigated because Gaedren is killable fast and the croc can't leave the pool. |
| Incapacitation | None. No save-or-die. |
| Flags | **Severe @L1**; **Crocodile Grab/Death Roll** + drowning if PCs are pulled under (warn melee PCs to fight from the walkways, not the water's edge). |

**Party-size adjustments (E8):**
| PCs | Budget (Severe) | Build |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | 90 | Gaedren (L2) + **Weak** Crocodile (L1) → ~90; or drop Gaedren to L1. |
| 4 | 120 | **As written** (Gaedren L2 + Crocodile L2). |
| 5 | 150 | Add a **Drain Spider (−1, +20)** boiling up from the pool, or **Elite** the Crocodile (+20). |
| 6 | 160 (Extreme cap for safety) | **Elite** Crocodile (L3, 80) + Gaedren (L2, 60) = 140; or add 2 Drain Spiders. |

### Chapter-1 attrition note
The fishery is a **no-rest dungeon** in PF1e (Yargin/Bloo raise the alarm). In PF2e this is milder (no Vancian slots), but Treat Wounds between fights is the pressure valve — if the party can't rest, the cumulative Low fights + Moderate spider nest + Severe boss is a meaningful first-session gauntlet. If Yargin reaches Gaedren, run E8 with Gaedren already in cover and Gobblegut pre-fed (enraged round 1).
