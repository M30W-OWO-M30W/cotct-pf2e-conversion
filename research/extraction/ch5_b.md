## Areas

### B2 Entry Corridor (line 11753)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "Rows of arrow slits line the eastern"
- **Encounter:** none stationed here normally; Watch Commander Lashton + skeletal nightmare + 12 skeletal champions (the B1 causeway force) fight here instead if PCs entered Scarwall by another route and skipped the causeway fight
- **DCs:** Reflex DC 15 half vs. 6d6 cold damage (oil cascade)
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Arrow slits east/west walls, murder holes above; far end blocked by heavy gate + iron portcullis; floor littered with orc skeletons (set dressing from the ancient orc assault)
  - Lashton waits here; when intruders are halfway across the causeway (B1) he signals skeletons in C1 above to open the gates so he and his minions can sally out
  - Round 1 of any combat in this room: skeletal guards in the room above dump troughs of supernatural freezing oil into the corridor — 6d6 cold to all creatures in the room, Reflex DC 15 half
  - Cramped quarters favor the PCs if the undead defend here
- **Tactics/morale:** skeletons fight intruders approaching from the north; cramped fighting

### B3 Watchposts (CR 8) (line 11771)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "Arrow slits in this room's longest wall"
- **Encounter:** 2x Scarwall Guard (undead minotaur skeletons, CR 6, XP 2,400, hp 61, statted on AP page 272) per room (rooms B3a and B3b)
- **DCs:** none
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Two mirrored rooms; stairs in B3a lead up to C2a, stairs in B3b lead up to C2b; no easy entry from B2
  - Guards were Kazavon's minotaur mercenaries (kept intruders out AND his own troops in), slain by Mandraivus, risen as undead
  - They fire on living creatures in the entry corridor (B2) through arrow slits
- **Tactics/morale:** defend B2 with ranged fire; do not pursue foes beyond the gatehouse

### B4 Death Chamber (CR 13) (line 11787)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "The scene in this large chamber is"
- **Encounter:** 1x Corpse Orgy CR 13 (XP 25,600; full stat block below)
- **DCs:** Fortitude DC 25 vs. absorb body; Fortitude DC 22 half vs. pain shriek 10d6 sonic (both in stat block)
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Room where Mandraivus's army made its longest stand vs. the orcs; centuries-old corpses kept fresh by a permanent gentle repose effect infusing any corpse in the room (necromantic auras in the walls)
  - The corpse mound in the NW corner IS the corpse orgy — an aberration that absorbs cadavers into its mass; trapped/sustained here by Scarwall's curse
  - It is demented: believes itself heir of Scarwall and sees living intruders as fragments of its own body returning; speaks Aklo, welcomes PCs "home," then scuttles forward to embrace/absorb them
- **Tactics/morale:** opens with pain shriek; relies on undead-like appearance so foes waste channel energy/undead-bane attacks (it's an aberration); flees deeper into Scarwall at ≤30 hp, fights to the death if cornered

### B5 Mandraivus's Fall (CR 14) (line 11869)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "Tattered bits of ruined tapestries line the"
- **Encounter:** Vortex of Madness haunt CR 10 (XP 9,600), then 1x Mandraivus the Lost CR 13 (XP 25,600) the round after the haunt collapses
- **DCs:** Strength break DC 30 (heavy iron crossbar barring the double door to B4 from the eastern side); Perception DC 25 notice haunt (faint battle sounds); Will DC 19 vs. haunt (confusion heightened to 6th); Fortitude DC 24 vs. Mandraivus's Con drain (ability drain)
- **Treasure:** Mandraivus's full plate — a suit of celestial armor bearing Lastwall heraldry, untouched on his remains
- **Beats:**
  - This is where Mandraivus (slayer of Kazavon) made his last stand and died; his anonymous skeleton in full plate slumps in the NE corner
  - Haunt: 2 rounds after a living creature enters, phantom battle cacophony builds into a vortex of shrieking spirits — DC 19 Will or confusion (heightened to 6th); haunt hp 20, proximity trigger w/ 2-round onset, reset 1 minute; destroyed if Scarwall's curse is lifted
  - Round after vortex collapses, Mandraivus rises from the armored bones as a unique advanced wraith — madness-consumed, only hatred remains
- **Tactics/morale:** (see stat block) targets paladins/armored fighters/clerics of militant or good gods; anyone wielding a bastard sword trumps all (screams "You shall not have Serithtial! It is mine and no other's!"); fights until destroyed

- **DCs:** none
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Two corridors: B6a (longer, twisting) and B6b; arrow slits overlook the keep's entry corridor
  - Bodies are Mandraivus's archers, gassed by orc alchemists during the B4 battle; harmless wisps of yellow smoke are a curse-maintained manifestation (eerie, no effect)
  - Bodies in B6b are harmless skeletons; six in B6a are juju zombies (clutching short swords) controlled by the trench mist suffusing floor/walls
  - All remain quiescent until anyone enters B6a; mist wells up amid the zombies and engulfs PCs, using amorphous to fit the hall
- **Tactics/morale:** once roused, zombies and mist pursue foes throughout the entire castle

### B15 Castle Kitchens (CR 13) (line 12161)

- **Beats:**
  - Kazavon executed victims by slow-roasting them (sometimes eating them) in the three huge ovens; Mandraivus threw the villainous cooks into the ovens — creating the haunt and the undead
  - Four humanoid outlines are burned into the oven-wall brick; ovens still faintly warm and smell of burning meat
  - Haunt triggers when anyone comes within 20 ft of the oven fronts: illusory sheet of flame + shrieking fire spirits; DC 20 Will disbelieves (no damage), failure = 10d6 fire; haunt hp 20, reset 1 hour, destroyed if curse lifted
  - Next round the scorched outlines peel off the wall and ignite as 4 cinder ghouls (spirits of the four cruelest cooks)
- **Tactics/morale:** ghouls attack closest targets; a ghoul adjacent to a foe at the start of its round attempts smoke inhalation while the others keep fighting; pursue into the larder (B16/southwest) but no farther; fight until destroyed

### B19 Malatrothe's Lair (CR 10) (line 12301)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "Innumerable bags of netting hang on the"
- **Encounter:** 1x Malatrothe, advanced night hag CR 10 (XP 9,600, hp 108; Bestiary 294, 215)

- **Treasure:** four soul jars (two filled with insane spirits purchased from Mithrodar, harvested via soul bind; soul jars detailed on AP page 436); no other treasure of note
- **Beats:**
  - Former apothecary/alchemist's chamber, redecorated Abaddon-style; brazier burns Styx-shore seaweed-charcoal (foul smell, no game effect)
  - Malatrothe is a recent arrival trading souls with Mithrodar (who shields her from the curse); wants to claim one of the chained spirit's anchoring spirits for Abaddon's soul markets
  - Con: if she hears battle, she disguises herself as "Alimae," a battered halfling herbalist "prisoner," and tries to lead PCs to C14 to sell them to Mithrodar in exchange for an anchor spirit
  - If exposed, she reverts with a cackle but still doesn't attack — proposes alliance instead: she helps the PCs defeat a spirit anchor so she can snatch its soul and flee to Abaddon
  - Intel she can share: Mithrodar's power is tied to four spirit anchors (he can't be destroyed while they exist); hundreds/thousands of spirits are bound in the walls; Castothrane, Belshallam, and Nihil are anchors; she knows the way to Belshallam's lair, prefers to take Nihil's soul, suspects (correctly) the fourth anchor lies in the chapel to the north
  - Betrayal clause: if she learns PCs intend to destroy Mithrodar himself (which would void her soul-harvest), she stays allied only until an anchor soul is caught, then warns Mithrodar seeking a second anchor as payment — Mithrodar kills her for it; PCs then find her body in C14, spirit tormented by his chains
  - Castle's dimensional lock blocks her etherealness while inside; may return to "dream haunt" PCs in the next adventure if she escapes
- **Tactics/morale:** treats PCs as dangerous; avoids melee, opens with magic missile and ray of enfeeblement at range; keeps a retreat path open; flees at <40 hp up to C19 (her loyal nightmare mount waits there), rides out onto parapet C15, goes ethereal once outside

### B23 Belshallam's Lair (CR 14) (line 12395)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "This large chamber, perhaps once a stable"
- **Encounter:** 1x Belshallam, adult umbral dragon CR 14 (XP 38,400, hp 195; Bestiary 2 102)
- **DCs:** DC 10 Perception (Belshallam, including -10 for sleeping) to wake from sounds of nearby combat or gargoyle shrieking from the minarets; DC 20 Sense Motive (after speaking with him at least 1 minute): notice a magical compulsion affects the dragon
- **Treasure:** none here (his hoard is B24)
- **Beats:**
  - Former kennel/stable for Kazavon's pets and mounts; now home of an umbral dragon lured by legends of Kazavon's hidden treasury
  - Trapped: when he destroyed one of Mithrodar's spirit anchors (the undead warrior Gorstav, since reanimated as a far weaker juju zombie), the chained spirit snared Belshallam as the replacement anchor; centuries of failed escapes have left him bored, listless, and increasingly dangerous
  - As a living anchor he gets limited mobility (a few miles radius) to hunt — eats wyverns/chimeras from the hills (orc and tarnworm meat unpalatable, sparing the Deadwatchers)
  - On noticing PCs he roars in delight and attacks for the thrill, BUT his true desire is freedom: if PCs disrupt Mithrodar's control (e.g., antimagic), he breaks off, screeches in victory, and flees — even abandoning his hoard
  - He knows dying here binds his soul to Scarwall forever: at <40 hp he turns craven, flees to his lair to hide/heal; cornered, he begs for mercy and offers his treasure as a bribe
  - Mithrodar's compulsion stops him from openly allying or explaining the anchor mechanism; if PCs say they're here to release the anchors, he goes very still and silent (his only way to signal they're on the right track); if PCs insist on killing him, he fights to kill
  - Development: if freed without being killed, he flies to a tor over the tarn; if PCs follow, he gratefully shares advice — GM mouthpiece for any hints about Scarwall's denizens/layout/perils, EXCEPT he knows nothing of the donjon or Star Tower and little of the guest wing; asked about Serithtial, he guesses donjon or Star Tower
- **Tactics/morale:** thrill-attacker; craven below 40 hp; flees instantly if anchor-bond broken
