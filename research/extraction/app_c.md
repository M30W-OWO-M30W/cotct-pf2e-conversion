## Afflictions & Rules Subsystems

### BLOOD VEIL (disease, line 19550)
- **Type:** disease, contact or injury; **Save** Fortitude DC 16
- First exposure: ethnically Varisian humanoids (humans, half-elves, half-orcs of Varisian parentage) have a flat 5% chance of total immunity
- **Onset** 1 day; **Frequency** 1/day; **Effect** 1d4 Con damage AND 1d4 Cha damage
- (No printed Cure line in this block; untreated it kills the average human in 7 days per lore text)
- Campaign exposure rule (Ch.2): PCs have 15% chance/day of exposure, +5% cumulative per day, capped at 50%. GM checks each morning for previous day; exposed PCs save vs Fortitude or take damage as incubation ends
- Origin: refined Vorel's phage (Rise of the Runelords ref), engineered by Urgathoan cultists funded by Red Mantis; no longer creates undead, more contagious; spread partly via infected coins
- Symptom progression (for flavor conversion): rash/blister "mask" on face → cough, buboes, pox blisters on neck/limbs → grape-sized blisters, black skin patches, coughing blood → death

### KYTON EIDOLON SUBTYPE (line 19564)
- For Unchained summoner; used by Shadowcount Sial (his eidolon Asyra, pg 456); GM may allow PCs
- Alignment LE. Base form: biped (improved natural armor, limbs [arms], limbs [legs])
- Gains access to tentacle and web evolutions (manifest as animated chains)
- 1st: resistance (cold) + skilled (Heal) evolutions; spiked chain proficiency
- 4th: +1 evolution pool
- 8th: unnerving gaze — 30 ft., 1 target/round (free action on its turn), Will negates or sickened 1 round; mind-affecting fear; DC Cha-based; immune to other kytons' gazes; summoner immune
- 12th: DR 5/good; gaze persists 1d3 rounds; up to 2 targets/round
- 16th: resistance (cold) → immunity (cold); gaze also staggers 1 round; up to 3 targets/round
- 20th: regeneration 5 (good weapons, good spells); still banished to Shadow Plane at 0 hp as normal

- Effect 1d2 Cha damage AND 1d2 Con DRAIN; Cure (OCR-garbled, reads "consecutive saves" — i.e., cure requires consecutive saves; count digit lost, likely 2)
