### Kazavon NPC entry (lines ~20651-20857)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none

Human — opens with mirror image + shield; haste only with allies; at ≤200 hp casts time stop then black tentacles, greater invisibility, wall of force; always Power Attacks in melee; fights to the death in human form (death transformation reverts him to dragon at 0 hp).

### Vencarlo Orisini / Blackjack NPC entry (lines 21735-21860)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** Vencarlo Orisini/Blackjack CR 10 (see Stat Blocks)
- **DCs:** DC 20 Perception: notice the two smallest fingers of his right hand never bend (he's missing them — lost in a duel with his student Grau, see p.33; gloves contain wooden lengths to disguise this)
- **Treasure:** see stat block gear (gold signet ring 250 gp; Blackjack gear set hidden in bag of holding)
- **Beats:**
  - Famous swordplay teacher and government critic; secretly the current Blackjack, a 200-year-old serial vigilante identity passed down one trainee per generation
  - Owns two rapiers: the +1 rapier carried openly; the +2 keen rapier (inherited from his mother) hidden with his vigilante gear in his bag of holding
  - Becoming Blackjack: PCs can find his gear in Ch.3 Part 1; if not, he approaches a chosen PC (usually between Ch.3 and Ch.4) to pass on the mantle; prefers privacy but accepts party-wide knowledge if all swear secrecy
  - While a PC wears Blackjack's cloak, mask, daggers, armor, boots, gloves, and rapier and honors the role: +10 circumstance bonus on Bluff, Diplomacy, and Intimidate checks against Korvosan citizens of non-noble status (explicitly includes Pilts Swastel)
  - The chosen PC gets a one-time rebuild: reallocate skill ranks, swap feats, and (if a vigilante) re-pick specialization and talents; GM may stretch the transition over a chapter, complete by the Deathhead Vault infiltration
  - Vencarlo then retires (keeps class levels, never uses vigilante talents again barring emergencies); a tailored encounter at the start of Ch.6 showcases the new Blackjack
- **Tactics/morale:** in social guise — fights defensively with +1 rapier, no lethal grace or hidden strike, tries to slip away to change identities. As Blackjack — aggressive, opens with disarm attempts; below 30 hp uses Combat Expertise defensively. Flees if outnumbered more than 3:1 (unless righting a great injustice) or below 10 hp.
