## Areas

### C. Pilts's Palace — overview (line 6048)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** garrison of Emperor's Thugs throughout palace (placed per area); Pilts can summon hundreds of replacements from Old Dock if guards are wiped out
- **DCs:**
  - DC 25 Diplomacy OR DC 16 Intimidate: convince guard thugs to admit PCs to see the emperor; beat the DC by 10+ → thugs do not demand PCs surrender weapons before escorting them to C4
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Palace = 6 wood buildings at 11 Silk Street, Old Dock; lower floors gutted by mob; only upper floors of northern buildings intact; southern buildings are roof-only shells; buildings stable (no collapse risk)
  - If escorted by a mob (per p.141), PCs enter at C1, are brought up to C4 to face the emperor; all thugs on alert
  - If unescorted, PCs pick their own route; any guard who notices them raises the alarm and demands their purpose
  - A guard battle can spread palace-wide; the emperor commands from his throne; defeating the emperor cows the entire mob (see C3) — no need to kill every thug

### Blood Pig! (line 6508)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none (rules section)
- **Encounter:** 8x Emperor's Thug CR 4 (Shinglesnipes; XP 1,200 each, hp 46, see p.142); 2x Wolverine CR 2 (XP 600 each, hp 22, Bestiary 279) in the pits; Piglets CR — (hp 4 each, young pig, Bestiary 3 291/113), as many as needed
- **DCs / mechanics (verbatim numbers):**
  - Goal: first team to 5 points; a point = getting a pig into your team's pit (PCs' pit C4f; Shinglesnipes' pit C4b)
  - Fresh pig loaded into the cage on the side opposite the scoring pit (C4a if PCs score into C4f; C4e if Shinglesnipes score into C4b)
  - Start: coin flip — heads = pig raised into cage C4a, tails = C4e; game starts 1 round later; everyone rolls initiative
  - Max 8 players per team; teams start in their squares (PCs C4d, Shinglesnipes C4c)
  - No weapons; no spellcasting before or during the match — each violation gives the opposing team a point; unarmed strikes legal (lethal or nonlethal); natural attacks legal this game only (banned in rematches as "cheaty claws")
  - Betting: thugs give 2-to-1 odds against the PCs; each PC may bet up to 100 gp
  - Picking up a pig: from a cage = standard action provoking AoO; a dropped live pig also requires an attack vs AC 15; thug who retrieves a pig tries to knock it out with an unarmed strike to ease carrying
  - Carrying: pig weighs 15 lbs; each round carrying a live conscious pig, roll 1d6 on Squirming Pig table
  - Dropping: taking damage while carrying a live pig → combat maneuver check, DC = 10 + 1 per point of damage taken, or drop it (dead pigs never dropped this way)
  - Dropped pig runs in a straight line away from the closest person on initiative count 0 each round, speed 30 ft; won't run off the roof or into a pit
  - Passing: standard action to a teammate within reach
  - Throwing: improvised thrown weapon, -4 attack penalty, 10-ft range increment; catching a deliberately thrown pig = DC 12 combat maneuver check
  - Throwing into a pit: ranged attack vs AC 6; dropping into an adjacent pit = free action, automatic
  - Intercepting: DC 18 combat maneuver check as an immediate action when a thrown pig passes a threatened square
  - Stealing: disarm combat maneuver; then roll on Squirming Pig table
  - Pits: 10 ft deep, 1-ft diameter reinforced opening (Tiny passes, Medium can't easily climb out); killing a wolverine = automatic forfeit for the killer's team; wolverine spends 1d3 rounds devouring a piglet, otherwise makes one claw attack per round vs creatures within 5 ft of the pit entrance
- **Treasure:** betting winnings only (2:1, up to 100 gp staked per PC)
- **Beats:**
  - Rules invented by a madman, never written down, change weekly — narrate Pilts announcing them; confusion is intended
  - Uses standard combat rules; the PCs aren't there to fight
- **Squirming Pig table (d6, line 6564):** 1 Slump (no action); 2 Squirm (carrier DC 12 Strength check or drop); 3 Squeal (crowd laughs); 4 Bite (+0 melee, 1d3-2 damage; damage may force a drop check); 5 Kick (carrier DC 12 Dexterity check or drop); 6 Panic (apply squeal + bite + squirm + kick at once)
- **Development (line 6628):** Pilts is a sore loser — on a PC win he demands "two out of three" and adds Jabbyr to the Shinglesnipes (even past the 8-player cap); after a second PC win he calls "three out of five" but is told they're out of pigs; he nearly guillotines the messenger, then congratulates the PCs and brings them to the balcony with Jabbyr and 2 thugs; PCs wait in C10 and meet a nervous Salvator under supervision; Salvator says nothing while Pilts lives (unless magically compelled or successfully intimidated); this confined moment is the PCs' best shot at Pilts away from most of his thugs
