### The Scarwall Curse (line 11328)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** none
- **DCs / mechanics:**
  - Ghost lights in windows: external manifestation only, no game effect; can never be examined closely (go dark first)
  - Self-repair: any structural damage fully repaired within a year, incrementally and never observably; no effect during the adventure unless PCs leave for months
  - Most spirits/undead are under the sway of the chained spirit (area C14), giving denizens efficient castle-wide watch; spirits entering the castle are assaulted/absorbed by him (affects shaman spirits [not spirit animals], spiritualist phantoms, Zellara — see Spiritual Doom)
  - Aura of Menace: casting detect evil or detect undead = no reading + DC 25 Will save or stunned 1 round; detect magic shows strong necromancy aura castle-wide (no stun)
  - Dread: sleeping in the castle requires DC 20 Will on waking to benefit from sleep; failure = fatigued and cannot prepare spells or regain slots; mind-affecting fear effect
  - Phantasms: one-time-per-location minor creepy manifestations targeting a PC; no actual game effect; creatures immune to phantasms can't be targeted; GM may omit
  - Soultrapping: living creature dying inside Scarwall: DC 23 Will or soul bound into the castle walls (as soul bind, CL 20th, structure instead of gem). To resurrect: dispel the soul bind on that soul (anywhere in castle if caster knows the name, else cast on corpse/fragment); resurrection must begin within 1 round of the dispel or the soul is reclaimed; dispel magic, greater dispel magic, dispel evil all work temporarily
  - Soultrapping (binding): any undead created in Scarwall is immediately bound (as hedged prison binding, no save); undead and living creatures remaining a week must save DC 22 Will or be bound; success = save again each additional week
  - Destroying the chained spirit frees all trapped souls and lifts the curse
  - Suppression: Serithtial (in area H4) has all powers suppressed; functions as a masterwork longsword even outside Scarwall until the curse is lifted (retains artifact near-indestructibility)
  - Unhallow: permanent unhallow over the whole castle, CL 20th; all inhabitants gain protection from good; DC to resist negative channeled energy +4, DC to resist positive energy -4; dimensional anchor effect on all non-inhabitants (does not block summoning); if dispelled, returns after 1d4 rounds

### Spiritual Doom (line 11368)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** none
- **DCs / mechanics:**
  - Zellara: if a PC bearing her harrow deck sets foot on the island or causeway, Mithrodar (C14) attacks her spirit within 2 rounds (gale wind, ghostly faces force her materialization). Each round: DC 25 Will save (her Will +7; the deck-bearer may substitute his own save). Failure = absorbed into the walls; deck becomes nonmagical; she can be saved only by lifting the curse. Leaving the island/causeway stops it; returning with the deck (even in an extradimensional container) restarts it — store the deck somewhere safe
  - Magic jar / possession / astral projection etc.: DC 15 Will on activation or the soul is absorbed (soul bind, CL 20th); body remains comatose (may die of thirst/starvation); dispelling the soul bind returns the soul to a living intact body. DC 25 Spellcraft while casting notices the danger in time to abandon (spell expended as if counterspelled, no soul bind exposure)
  - Shaman: feels the taint on entry; must succeed at DC 15 caster level check to prepare spells or bond a wandering spirit in the castle; failure = no retry for 24 hours
  - Speak with dead / speak with haunt: automatically fails; caster instead assaulted by cacophonous sound (audible only to caster): DC 20 Fortitude or permanently deafened
  - Spiritualist: phantom not absorbed (bond protects) but takes -2 on attack rolls and saving throws while the curse persists; if the phantom is banished to the Ethereal Plane inside the castle, the spiritualist gains a negative level (DC 15 Fortitude removes it after 24 hours); a banished phantom can't return to the spiritualist's consciousness inside the castle while the curse is active (an already-active phantom can enter freely, with the penalties)

### Defeating Scarwall's Curse (line 11386)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** none
- **DCs / mechanics:**
  - DC 25 Spellcraft (studying the necromantic aura): discern that remove curse / break enchantment cannot remove the curse
  - Curse persists via chained spirit Mithrodar (area C14, stats p. 468), anchored to four "spirit anchors": umbral dragon Belshallam (B23), skeletal champion Castothrane (D1), devil Nihil (D14), demilich Zev Ravenka (F11). While all four exist, Mithrodar cannot be truly defeated; defeat all four, then destroy Mithrodar, to lift the curse. Certain spells can also sever an anchor (see chained spirit's Spirit Anchor ability, p. 468)
  - 1 anchor defeated: Dread effect ends (safe sleep); castle issues silent scream alerting all undead; all Scarwall undead gain channel resistance +4 (stacks +4 on existing channel resistance) until all four anchors fall
  - 2 anchors defeated: Aura of Menace ends; detect evil/undead work; shamans and spiritualists no longer hampered
  - 3 anchors defeated: wall spirits can no longer roam; greater shadows, spectres, wraiths removed from random encounters (treat rolls as no encounter)
  - 4 anchors defeated: unhallow and dimensional lock vanish; Mithrodar can now be permanently defeated; speak with dead/haunt, magic jar, possession work normally
  - Replacing anchors: Mithrodar can chain new non-mindless evil creatures as replacement anchors (p. 468); Ildervok (G7) is immune (bound by Zon-Kuthon's will, not the curse); constructs (e.g., golem in C4) can't serve; Laori or Sial could become anchors; curse effects return in reverse order as new anchors are chained
