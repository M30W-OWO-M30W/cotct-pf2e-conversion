### A89 Venster's Boxroom (CR 6) (line 16212)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "This dusty room contains a bed, a large" (boxed text)
- **Encounter:** Venster Arabasti, tiefling ghost (CR 6) — initially a weak misty manifestation, non-hostile
- **DCs:** none in-area (see A88 for entry)
- **Treasure:** none listed (harrow deck in redwood case is scenery; Zellara's deck is the item transformed later)
- **Beats:**
  - Temperature drops; mist rises from harrow deck and forms a horned old man — Venster's ghost.
  - He begs the PCs to fetch his bones from area A11 (castle dungeons); until then he can barely manifest and fades after a short time, re-manifesting only every few hours.
  - Zellara empathically senses where the bones lie once Venster manifests, and can guide the deck-bearer to A11.
  - Development (bones returned): Venster manifests fully; reveals Ileosa poisoned Eodred II (he shares guilt); reveals her plan — ritual at the "Sunken Queen" using the Everdawn Pool and the lifeblood of thousands of unwitting Korvosan "supplicants" to gain eternal youth; urges PCs to stop her; points them to Domina's study (A90).
  - Final gift: Venster + Zellara channel the psychic energy of Korvosa's dead through Zellara's harrow deck, transforming it into a *harrow deck of many things* (see p.426). Some infused spirits are cruel/insane; the ghosts moderate them through harrow tradition.
  - After draws are done, Zellara manifests one last time, kisses the deck-bearer, and passes on; the deck becomes a normal nonmagical deck. Unspent harrow points from this chapter's reading remain usable, but no further special harrow readings.
  - Both ghosts move on to the Boneyard even if no PC draws from the deck.
- **Tactics/morale:** If attacked, Venster fights with telekinesis (shoving intruders out) and corrupting touch; fights until destroyed, rejuvenates in 2d6 rounds and does not re-attack unless attacked again. Permanent destruction tied to Development (laying him to rest / Ileosa's death — he is bound here by guilt while Ileosa lives).

### B THE SUNKEN QUEEN — overview (line 16481)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "Surrounded by a grove of primeval mangroves and" (boxed arrival text)
- **Encounter:** site-wide defenders: Beirawash (B1), 6 boggard champions (B7/B8), 4 erinyes furies (B16), Ithier (B15), Ileosa + 6 false Ileosas + taniniver (B20)
- **DCs / structure rules:**
  - DC 40 Perception: spot third-level air vents from outside (gaseous entry route)
  - Walls: hardness 24, 1,620 hp per 5-ft square (tripled), *ghost touch* property — no incorporeal/ethereal passage
  - Teleport within/into/out of: DC 32 caster level check or spell fails and the teleporter takes 20d6 damage (DC 24 Fortitude half); creatures that bathed in the Everdawn Pool (Ileosa + 4 furies) are exempt
  - Floor tilts 20 degrees SE: walking requires DC 15 Acrobatics (balancing = flat-footed); fail by 5+ = fall prone, slide SE into a wall for 1d6 damage
  - Ceilings: 50 ft in chambers, 25 ft in passages
- **Treasure:** none here (see B2, B3, etc.)
- **Beats:**
  - Thassilonian pyramid of Runelord Sorshen, site of the Everdawn Pool; sank into the Mushfens, resting at 20 degrees on bedrock; one of three metallic horns has collapsed; south face bears a giant relief of a naked standing woman (Sorshen).
  - Moat-like murky pond, 40 ft deep on the deepest side; original entrance found ~40 ft underwater; Ileosa cut a smaller second-level side entrance hidden by Togomor's permanent *illusory wall* (CL 16th).
  - Beirawash the devilfish (worshiped as a god by local boggards) and several boggard champions were charmed by Ileosa; boggards now call her "Mother Queen," the living incarnation of the carving.
  - Level 1 fully flooded (SE buried in silt); level 2 partially flooded; levels 3-4 dry and climate-controlled by ancient magic; no stairs between floors — only open shafts (flight/swim/climb needed).
  - While attuning in B20, Ileosa has unlimited *clairaudience/clairvoyance* sensors throughout the complex with *true seeing* through them (Stealth can still beat her); on noticing PCs she alerts her four furies, who telepathically rally all defenders.
  - Six boggard champions (barbarians from regional tribes, recruited via *charm monster*, now loyal) intercept on command.
