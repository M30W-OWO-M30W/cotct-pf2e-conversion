### A 'Friendly' Game of Sredna (line 8602, minigame)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** opposed-check minigame vs. Krojun
- **DCs / rules:**
  - Two contestants on hands and knees, bound head-to-head by a leather loop; loser bows head.
  - Rounds 1–3: opposed Intimidate checks; winner gains 2 points; tie = 1 point each. Pulling before round 4 = disqualification.
  - Round 4+: roll initiative; each turn choose Tug (opposed Strength check; defender who dug in last turn gets +4; tugger wins = 2 points, tugger loses/ties = opponent gains 2 points) or Dig In (+4 on next Strength check to resist a tug).
  - End of each round after the 4th: each contestant attempts a Fortitude save, DC = opponent's current point total; failure = collapse/loss; both fail = draw; Endurance feat grants +4 on this save.
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Krojun rages if the match passes 6 rounds, or responds in kind to a raging PC.
- **Respect Points:** force Krojun to rage +1; PC loses +1; draw +2; PC wins +3.
- **Story Award:** 6,400 XP if PCs earn 2+ Respect Points from the game (even on a loss).

### A2 Pool of the Havero (CR 10+) (line 8914)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "The air in this massive, cathedral-like space"
- **Encounter:** Havero tentacles (each hp 66, see p. 473; the havero itself is CR 24 and cannot wake or come through — p. 472)
- **DCs:** DC 30 Perception: locate concealed observation gaps in north/south walls (to A3 etc.). DC 21 Survival: track the Sklar-Quah across this floor toward A3.
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Former primary summoning chamber, now a 15-ft-deep pool of cold stagnant water over the petrified-looking flank of a hibernating havero (~10 ft uneven depth of rubbery flesh with closed bulbous eyes); bottom is dimensionally "adjacent" to the creature orbiting a dead star; 60-ft vaulted ceiling, 5-ft balcony ring, central bridge, 4 Sihedron doors (large S and E, small NW and SW).
  - **Noise Points table:** verbal-component spell 1/spell; running (or swimming) 1/moving character; yelling 1/yell; combat 5/round; sonic effect 10/effect; attacking tentacles 0; attacking the havero's flank 1 per point of damage. Noise generated IN A2 is doubled (except attacks on the havero). Total decays 1d10 per minute. Reductions never trigger new reactions.
  - **Tentacle reaction thresholds** (effects trigger the round after the total reaches each level): 10 = Twitch (ruin shakes, pool churns); 20 = Investigate (1 tentacle emerges from one of the two central squares on a random side of the pool); 40 = Assault (as Seek, but 2 tentacles emerge from the pool-edge square nearest the last noise); 50 = Wrath (4 tentacles emerge). (OCR note: the table's "Seek" row — ~30 points — is partially garbled in the source; Assault references it.)
  - **Tentacle mechanics:** each tentacle is a unique creature; max 4 active at once; cannot move its base but has 60-ft reach (10 ft used exiting the pool); on its action the tip relocates anywhere within 50 ft of its base and makes one attack vs. any creature adjacent to any point along its length; it can be attacked anywhere along its length (track with token trails/string).
  - Tentacle at 0 hp retracts; reduce Noise total by 1d20 per defeated tentacle; recompute active tentacles at start of next round; dropping below 10 retracts all and quells the havero for 1 hour (no noise rouses it). Killing 20+ tentacles in one combat also quells it for 1 hour.
  - Red Mantis here: observe, won't initiate; if the havero wakes they watch, striking only if noticed or once tentacles withdraw while PCs are wounded; if no PCs present, they avoid the pool and search rooms in numerical order.
  - Sklar-Quah here: uneasy, move on to A3; aid the PCs in this room only if things look dire.
- **Story Award:** 9,600 XP for navigating the room without waking the havero, or for quelling its tentacles after waking it (regardless of tentacles destroyed).
