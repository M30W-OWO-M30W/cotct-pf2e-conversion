### B30 Bath (CR 13) (line 12599)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "A large pool of fouled water fills much"
- **Encounter:** 6x Festering Spirit CR 8 (XP 4,800 each, hp 58 each, Bestiary 4 98)
- **DCs:** none printed (disease exposure has no DC stated here)
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Former guest bathing area; Kazavon sometimes used it as an abattoir.
  - Pool only 3 ft deep; water foul/rancid — drinking it exposes the drinker to blinding sickness.
  - Spirits of guests killed during the Order's attack fused into the slime as 6 festering spirits.
  - They lurk in the water; rise to attack the first person who interacts with the pool, or as soon as the group tries to leave the room.
- **Tactics/morale:** Do not pursue foes out of this chamber.

### B34 Malice Aforethought (CR 10) (line 12671)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** "This area appears to have been a parlor"
- **Encounter:** none (cursed statue hazard)
- **DCs:**
  - DC 25 Knowledge (history): identify the unveiled statue as Kazavon in human form
  - DC 22 Will: resist curse compelling victim to climb the ladder in B35 into the Prison of Woe and remain there
  - Break DC 30 / hardness 8 / hp 80: destroy the statue (renders curse ineffective)
  - DC 22 Will: each time a creature damages the statue, save or be affected by feeblemind (CL 20th)
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Marble statue of a man in noble robes, head covered by a brittle leather sack that crumbles if jostled/removed; removing it reveals Kazavon's human face.
  - Curse (mind-affecting compulsion, CL 20th) affects anyone who looks on the face; compulsion ends 1 minute after victim stops looking, but re-looking forces a new save; physical restraint or curse-breaking magic stops it.
  - Only one creature can be statue-cursed at a time; a new victim frees the previous one. Any number can suffer the feeblemind effect.
  - Mandraivus's group bagged the statue after divining its purpose; Kazavon used it as a round-robin prisoner game for guests.
  - Worshipers of Zon-Kuthon are immune to this curse.
- **Story Award:** 9,600 XP if the PCs avoid or overcome the curse (statue destruction not required).

  - Count Andachi's harmless shade then manifests (continually flayed/restored apparition); instantly recognized if a PC carries his skull (D14) or signet ring (C11). Speech anchor: "You have done a great thing today. You" — paraphrase: he confesses summoning Kazavon via Zon-Kuthon to defend Tamrivena, his death (skinned alive and eaten), Mandraivus's victory with Serithtial and later murder by orcs; he senses Kazavon quickening in Queen Ileosa back in Korvosa (names Korvosa, Ileosa, Sabina, and the PCs); the PCs must recover Serithtial — it cannot be destroyed or taken far without invoking Iomedae's wrath, but a fallen agent of the Midnight Lord still subdues/hides it in a vault deep below, accessible via the Star Tower (Kazavon's inner sanctum); seek the blade's crypt. He then fades; the House of Tamrivena ends.
- **Tactics/morale:** Mithrodar commands much necromantic magic but cannot directly influence events elsewhere in Scarwall; fears losing his anchors.
- **Story Award:** 25,600 XP for defeating all four spirit anchors and destroying Mithrodar (lifting the curse).
