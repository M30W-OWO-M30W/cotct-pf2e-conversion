### Divining with a Harrow Deck (line 18360)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** none
- **DCs:** none
- **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - GM performs a harrowing as ghostly Zellara near the start of each chapter; use Deluxe Harrow Deck prop, or playing cards/dice substitute.
  - Step 1 — The Choosing: pull the 9 cards of the current chapter's suit; each player randomly draws one; record it. Card matching alignment/highest ability = fortunate; oppositional alignment/lowest ability = ominous. Cards returned and deck reshuffled. The Choosing card grants 1 Harrow Point plus an automatic boon in one specific encounter that chapter.
  - Step 2 — The Spread: draw 9 cards face down in 3×3 grid; positions map to the nine alignments and past/present/future.
  - Step 3 — The Past: left column (also = law); reveal top to bottom; interpret vs PC backgrounds/prior chapters.
  - Step 4 — The Present: center column (= neutrality); interpret vs current situation in Korvosa.
  - Step 5 — The Future: right column (= chaos); interpret vs upcoming chapter events; leave all 9 cards on table.
  - Step 6 — Calculate Harrow Points: each PC gains Harrow Points = number of spread cards of the chapter's suit, +1 for their Choosing card (minimum 1 guaranteed), +1 more if their Choosing card appeared in the spread. Unspent points are lost at chapter end.
- **Tactics/morale:** n/a

### Interpreting a Spread (line 18384)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** none / **DCs:** none / **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Misalignment rule: evil-aligned card in top row (good row) or good-aligned card in bottom row (evil row) = misaligned; use misaligned meaning. Cards neither good nor evil can never be misaligned.
  - Interpretation is pure flavor; only mechanic is Harrow Point count. Keep readings to one sentence per card.
  - True Match: card alignment = placement alignment → extreme importance.
  - Opposite Match: card alignment exactly opposite its placement (e.g. CG card in LE position) → always misaligned, momentous.
  - Partial Match: one alignment component matches placement → increased importance; can sometimes be misaligned.
  - Choosing card appearing in spread → interpret for that specific player, not the party.

### Harrowing Without a Deck (line 18404)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** none / **DCs:** none / **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Playing-card substitute: write the 54 harrow card names on a standard deck including both jokers.
  - Dice substitute: per "card", roll 1d10 for alignment (1=LG, 2=LN, 3=LE, 4=NG, 5=N, 6=NE, 7=CG, 8=CN, 9=CE, 10=reroll) and 1d6 for ability (1=Str, 2=Dex, 3=Con, 4=Int, 5=Wis, 6=Cha).
  - With dice, record results and placements publicly on a large sheet.

### Harrow Points — general rules (line 19230)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** none / **DCs:** none / **Treasure:** none
- **Beats:**
  - Spending a Harrow Point is a free action; no per-round limit while points remain.
  - After each chapter's harrowing, tell players the chapter's spend options.
  - Choosing-card boons activate automatically for the drawing PC when the indicated encounter begins and last for that encounter; do not reveal boons in advance.

### Harrow Deck of Many Things (line 19408)
- **Read-aloud anchor:** none
- **Encounter:** possible (card-triggered): elder air elemental (The Cyclone); immolation devil, Bestiary 2 p. 87 (The Fiend); tataka rakshasa, Bestiary 3 p. 230 (The Rakshasa); 1d4+2 fiendish shambling mounds (The Tangled Briar); 1d6 opposite-alignment PC duplicates (The Waxworks); mirror-of-opposition PC duplicate at Ileosa's side (The Twin); formian myrmarch ally, Bestiary 4 p. 109 (The Queen Mother)
- **DCs:** DC 35 Knowledge (arcana or history) to recognize the ghost as Sorshen, Runelord of Lust (The Teamster card, line 19062 block at 19459); The Snakebite touch poison Fort save DC = 10 + character's HD + Con modifier (greenblood oil)
- **Treasure:** The Demon's Lantern soul gem worth character level × 2,500 gp; The Empty Throne 15,000 gp inheritance (player-spent on Ultimate Equipment items, GM approval); The Foreign Trader 20,000 gp magic-item credit per age category advanced; The Paladin grants a *holy avenger* (or permanent +2 to one ability score if wielding *Serithtial*); The Wanderer turns one mundane possession into a GM-chosen magic item worth 20,000 gp or less; The Keep grants a permanent personal demiplane (*create demiplane*, UM 213); The Winged Serpent grants one *wish*
- **Beats:**
  - Near campaign's end, Zellara's deck can transform into a *harrow deck of many things*; each PC declares up to 4 draws.
  - Spirit-stacked deck: if a PC draws an undesirable card, any PC may spend 1 Harrow Point to discard it and redraw (does not reduce declared draw count).
  - When a new PC begins drawing, all used/discarded cards are reshuffled in. After all PCs finish, the deck becomes nonmagical.
  - Base rules are in Artifacts & Legends pp. 24–27, but this AP replaces all results; cards affect only the drawing PC, never the environment.
- **All 54 draw effects (verbatim mechanics, terse):**
  - **The Avalanche:** character is imprisoned (*imprisonment* spell or a powerful being).
  - **The Bear:** gains the lycanthropy corruption (Horror Adventures 28).
  - **The Beating:** while in combat, always considered flanked.
  - **The Betrayal:** animal companion/familiar/mount/cohort becomes alienated and forever hostile; if none, a key allied NPC (e.g. Vencarlo, Cressida, Trinia) betrays the PC and appears at Ileosa's side at the climax.
  - **The Big Sky:** 1/day +10 on combat maneuver checks or to CMD for 1 round; a nearby metal object shatters when used.
  - **The Brass Dwarf:** immunity to one energy type of her choice; vulnerability to another energy type of the GM's choice.
  - **The Carnival:** set aside; GM draws 9 cards, shows them face up, flips and shuffles them; PC selects one and takes its effect (sharp-eyed players can track a desired card).
  - **The Courtesan:** PC's favorite item (preferably a magic weapon) becomes intelligent (Core Rulebook p. 532 random generation). If wielding *Serithtial*: instead draw three new cards and pick one to apply, discarding the other two.
  - **The Cricket:** may draw up to 3 additional cards; base land speed permanently +10 ft per additional card drawn (in addition to those cards' effects).
  - **The Crows:** choose between most valuable item or a major ally (GM's choice); the unchosen is destroyed/slain beyond any mortal restoration.
  - **The Cyclone:** drawn into an arena chamber ringed by ghosts; must solo-fight an elder air elemental; failure to win within 1d6+1 rounds → instantly transported to the Elemental Plane of Air.
  - **The Dance:** permanently roll initiative twice, take either result.
  - **The Demon's Lantern:** body disintegrates; items remain plus a glowing soul gem worth level × 2,500 gp; *miracle*, *resurrection*, *true resurrection*, or *wish* restores the PC (destroying the gem).
  - **The Desert:** one-time free-action instant transport to any known location on the same plane with up to 10 allies and 2,000 lbs of goods; ignores all teleport barriers.
  - **The Eclipse:** from dusk to dawn, treated as 1 level lower; multiclass PC picks the affected class on draw.
  - **The Empty Throne:** urge (not compulsion) to sit on the Crimson Throne; if done, reality warps — everyone but the PCs remembers the PC as Korvosan aristocracy; manor + servants (GM); 15,000 gp inheritance spent by player on Ultimate Equipment items (GM approval).
  - **The Fiend:** Lorthact the Unraveler (Inner Sea Bestiary 26–27) marks the PC for destruction; an immolation devil (Bestiary 2 87) joins Ileosa at the Sunken Queen and delivers the warning.
  - **The Foreign Trader:** trade years of life for treasure — advance any number of age categories (Core Rulebook 169), taking all age penalties and none of the benefits; gain 20,000 gp credit per category for non-unique magic items; unspent credit lost; years restorable only by deific intervention; no deal for ageless characters.
  - **The Forge:** must reforge one owned armor or weapon into another suit/weapon of equal or lesser gp value; excess value lost.
  - **The Hidden Truth:** one-time free action: an omniscient spirit answers a question or solves a puzzle.
  - **The Idiot:** Cha, Int, and Wis each reduced by 1d4 (roll separately).
  - **The Inquisitor:** one-time: force a creature to truthfully answer a single question; no insight granted; wasted if the creature is genuinely ignorant.
  - **The Joke:** PC selects another PC; GM draws and reveals 3 new cards; the selected PC picks one and gains its effects; the original PC picks one of the remaining two; last card discarded.
  - **The Juggler:** +2 to two ability scores of his choice, then must swap those two scores with one another.
  - **The Keep:** permanent personal demiplane (*create demiplane*, Ultimate Magic 213).
  - **The Liar:** favorite magic item gains a curse — roll on Table 15-27: Common Item Curses (Core Rulebook 537).
  - **The Locksmith:** one-time: open any one door, lock, bindings, or barrier, including conditional magic gates/portals; at the Sunken Queen the PC knows it can suppress the anti-teleport restriction there for 24 hours.
  - **The Lost:** cannot gain another level in his current highest-level class; bypassed by dying and returning — but the next return to life works as *reincarnate* regardless of the actual spell used.
  - **The Marriage:** a comely genie proposes; acceptance requires a lavish ceremony within the week and lifelong commitment; declining/breaking the vow outrages the genie's elemental court (GM adjudicates retribution).
  - **The Midwife:** gains exactly enough XP to reach the next level.
  - **The Mountain Man:** grows one size category; equipment does not resize.
  - **The Mute Hag:** permanently blind, deaf, or mute (player's choice); curable only by *miracle* or *wish*.
